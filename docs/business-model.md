# Business Model

## Core SaaS Model

WorkflowGuard AI is a self-service audit product for n8n and AI automation workflows. The core value is a fast, understandable production-readiness report before a workflow touches customers, revenue, or regulated data.

These pricing notes are hypotheses, not guarantees. They should be validated with real user behavior before implementation.

## Free Local Audit

The free path should remain simple:

- Upload workflow JSON.
- Run a local or hosted audit.
- See headline scores and the most important issues.
- Understand safety boundaries clearly.

## Paid Report Export

Hypothesis: 9-19 EUR per export.

A paid report can add:

- Exportable client-ready PDF or HTML report.
- Deeper remediation guidance.
- Before-and-after comparison.
- Workflow owner and release metadata.
- Team handoff checklist.

## Pro Subscription

Hypothesis: 19-49 EUR per month.

A subscription can add:

- Saved audit history.
- Re-audit tracking.
- Shared workspaces.
- Reviewer comments.
- Policy templates.
- More detailed rule packs.

## Template Store

Hypothesis: 19-99 EUR per template pack.

A template store can package safe n8n templates, audit-ready starter workflows, and production checklists. Free templates can drive trust; paid templates can monetize repeatable workflow patterns.

## Boilerplates

Hypothesis: 99-499 EUR per boilerplate.

Future boilerplates can include:

- n8n production workflow starter kits.
- AI automation safety checklists.
- Client handoff report templates.
- Agency delivery packs.

## Marketplace Distribution

Templates and boilerplates can be distributed through a public template library, partner communities, and relevant no-code marketplaces once privacy, support, and licensing rules are clear.

## Future AI Platform Apps

WorkflowGuard AI can become one product inside a broader set of automation reliability tools. Future apps should share audit primitives where useful, but each app must earn its own scope.

## Future Data Products

Future data products may include anonymized trend reports about automation failure modes. This should only happen with explicit consent, strong anonymization, and clear privacy policies.

## Portfolio Expansion Order

1. WorkflowGuard AI first.
2. Workflow and template store second.
3. Boilerplates third.
4. AI platform app fourth.
5. Data products fifth.
6. Trading and clothing remain secondary labs.

## Explicitly Not In Scope Yet

- Authentication.
- Payments.
- External persistence.
- Analytics.
- Workflow execution.
- Permanent uploaded workflow storage.
- Importing directly from n8n Cloud or GitHub.
